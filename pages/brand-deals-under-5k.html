How to Get Brand Deals with
Under 5k Followers

The step-by-step roadmap for micro-creators to secure high-paying sponsorships and launch their careers.

One of the biggest myths in the creator economy is that you need 100,000 followers to start working with brands. For years, massive accounts dominated sponsorships. But today, the game has changed. Brands have realized that micro-creators (accounts with 1,000 to 10,000 followers) have something big accounts lack: authentic trust and high engagement.

In fact, micro-influencers boast average engagement rates of 3% to 7%, compared to less than 1.5% for accounts with over 100k followers. For brands, this means better conversions and higher return on investment (ROI).

1. Optimize Your Bio for Brands

Before a brand agrees to work with you, they will audit your page. Your profile needs to scream professionalism from the very first second:

  • Clear Niche: State exactly what you make content about (e.g., "Helping tech enthusiasts find the best gadgets" or "Sartorial fashion for petite men").
  • Contact Email: Place a professional business email directly in your bio or contact button (e.g., collabs@yourname.com instead of a personal email account).
  • Location: Brands need to know your geographic market to ensure your audience matches their target demographics.

2. Build a Clean 1-Page Media Kit

A media kit is your creator resume. As a micro-influencer, you don't need a 10-page slide deck. A clean 1-page PDF is perfect:

  • Short Bio: Introduce your brand and mission.
  • Audience Stats: Highlight your demographic breakdown (age, gender, top locations) and your engagement rate.
  • Visual Examples: Show screenshots of your highest-performing posts.

3. Master the Outreach Pitch

Do not wait for brands to DM you. Take initiative and pitch them via email. Here is the exact template we use at GM-Trendify to help our micro-creators land deals:

Subject: Collab Proposal: [Your Name] x [Brand Name]

Hi [Brand Manager Name],

I've been a huge fan of [Brand Name] and regularly use your [Specific Product] in my content creation routine.

I am a content creator in the [Your Niche] space, with an audience of [Follower Count] highly engaged followers. My audience is actively looking for recommendations on [Niche Topic], and my average post gets a [Your Engagement Rate]% engagement rate.

I would love to pitch a video partnership showcasing how [Brand Name] helps solve [Common Creator/User Problem].

I have attached my 1-page media kit below. Let me know if you would be open to exploring a collaboration!

Best,
[Your Name]
